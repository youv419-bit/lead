🎯 The Client Conversion Blueprint
Everything you need to turn a cold lead into a paying client — even with no prior relationship
1
Research Before You Reach Out
Visit their website. Note 3 specific problems: slow load time, broken mobile layout, outdated design, missing trust signals. Mention these in your first message. Generic outreach gets ignored. Specific insights get replies.
2
Lead With Value, Not Your Services
Don't say "I'm a Shopify developer". Say "I noticed your product images are loading in 8 seconds — I can fix that in 48 hours, here's proof from a similar store I improved." Results first, pitch second.
3
The Perfect Subject Line Formula
Use: "[Their Company] — quick question about [specific issue]". Example: "PetStore.com — quick question about your checkout page". Curiosity + specificity = high open rates. Never use "Web Development Services".
4
Use Social Proof Immediately
Include one result from a past project: "I helped a UK fashion brand increase their Shopify conversion rate from 1.2% to 3.8% in 6 weeks." Numbers are powerful. If you're new, offer a free audit to build credibility.
5
Always Include a Low-Risk CTA
Don't ask for a call immediately. Instead: "Would it be helpful if I sent you a free 5-point audit of your store?" This is easy to say yes to. Once they say yes, you have a warm conversation started.
6
Follow Up 3–5 Times
80% of deals close after follow-up #3–5. Most freelancers give up after 1. Space them: Day 1, Day 4, Day 9, Day 16, Day 25. Each follow-up should add new value — a tip, a case study, a relevant article.
7
LinkedIn Strategy for Agencies
Connect without a note. Wait 24h. Then send: "Hi [Name], I help [industry] businesses on Shopify improve [specific metric]. Would a quick audit be useful?" Simple, non-pushy. Works well on agency owners.
8
Pricing: Anchor High, Offer Options
Always present 3 packages: Basic, Standard, Premium. Anchor with Premium first (£3000+). Most clients choose middle. Never quote one fixed price — it becomes a yes/no decision instead of a choice.
